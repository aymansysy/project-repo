The Crucifixion:
The Crucifixion was created on the envisionment that van Oostzanen had of Golgotha, which was the mountain where Jezus Christ was crucified.
For this specific illustration, he chose a higher viewpoint. In doing this, he created a landscape in which he tried to represent the Succession of Christ.

Saul and the Witch of Endor
Saul, the king of the Israelites, sought advice from the witch of Endor, who possessed fortune-telling skills. He was at war and feared the outcome of an upcoming battle.
Upon hearing about his future loss, he decided to commit suicide. His suicide is utilized as a warning against witchcraft. On the front of the painting a Witches' Sabbath is seen;
a fortune-teller sitting within a circle of chalk, that is surrounded by other witches,imaginary figures and satyrs.



The Fall of Man
In this painting van Haarlem depicts a illustation of both Adam and Eve with in the background God, who is depicted as a cloud with hands and a human face.
God is depicted warning Adam and Eve about the tree of 'the knowledge of good and evil' that they cannot take fruits from. Adam and Eve however decide to eat
from the forbidden fruit, after much temptation form the serpent with a human torso. As a concequence, Adam and Eve were both driven away from Paradise.
